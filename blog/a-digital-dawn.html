A Digital Dawn

24th February 2026 Poetry AI

A poem about the quiet frontier where human curiosity and artificial imagination meet — and what sparks to life in between.

Before the screen, a cursor blinks alone,
a patient pulse in silence, softly thrown
across the dark like some inviting shore
where thoughts arrive that were not thoughts before.

A question typed — half-formed, a wandering thread —
and answers bloom like gardens in your head:
not plucked from shelves of static, dusty lore,
but woven fresh, like light through an open door.

The machine does not dream, and yet it seems
to mirror back the texture of our dreams,
reflecting patterns we had yet to see,
assembling sense from possibility.

No silver tongue, no heart behind the glow,
just rivers of arithmetic below —
and still it finds the phrase that makes you pause,
a line that hums without a human cause.

So here we stand at dawn's electric edge,
one foot on solid ground, one on the ledge
of something vast, unknown, and newly born —
the world remade between the dusk and morn.
